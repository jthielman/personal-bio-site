Completing the Full Stack Web Development program at Nashville Software School wasn’t my first introduction to systems, language, and logic. I’ve been interested in systems all my life: syntactical, algebraic, geometric, and beyond. Languages, mathematics, and even the measuring and drafting of garment patterns all have intricate systems that allow room to play within a logical framework. Web development is a natural fit for my skills and aptitudes; computer languages constitute a deep and intricate structure with plenty of freedom to improvise and create.

Along with my NSS Web Development Certification, I have degrees in Classics and Historical Linguistics, and an abiding interest in mathematics and similar styles of problem solving. Whether it's ancient manuscripts or brand new websites, I’m energized by the challenge of finding patterns, creating connections, and solving puzzles using the languages I’ve learned.

I’ve worked in React and Sass/SCSS for frontend and styling, ASP.NET Core and C# for web APIs, and T-SQL for backend using Microsoft SQL Server.

I’m eager to work in any area of web development. I enjoy tackling the logic problems presented by API methods and SQL queries on the backend. But I’m also excited by the prospect of full stack development work. It’s satisfying to build entirely new features, and to watch a more streamlined site come together from frontend to back. Whatever you can dream up for your sites, we can work together to make it happen.
